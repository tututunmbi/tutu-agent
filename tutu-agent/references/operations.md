## 4. Constraint Taxonomy (Detailed)

When debriefing engagements, map every client to one or more of these 7 constraints. Over time, patterns will emerge that inform content, frameworks, and the 2nd Brain.

1. **Creative Quality Dependency** — The founder IS the quality control. Nothing ships without their eyes on it. The business can't grow because the founder can't clone their taste.

2. **Revenue Concentration** — Too few clients and/or project-based revenue creates feast-or-famine cycles. No predictability. Constant anxiety between projects.

3. **Talent Retention** — Creative businesses lose their best people because of poor systems: unclear growth paths, chaotic workflows, no SOPs, founder chaos that burns people out.

4. **Operational Chaos** — Every project feels like the first time. No documentation, no repeatable processes, tribal knowledge only. Scaling is impossible because there's nothing to scale.

5. **Identity Crisis** — The founder's personal brand and the company brand are tangled. The company can't exist without the founder's name. This blocks delegation, hiring, and eventually selling.

6. **Scaling Bottleneck** — Demand exists but growth is blocked because the founder is in every meeting, every decision, every client relationship. Revenue plateaus despite opportunity.

7. **Strategic Void** — No roadmap beyond "do good creative work." Reactive, not proactive. No clear direction for where the business is going. Drifting.

## 5. Key Frameworks to Build (2nd Brain Priority)

These are the operational patterns from Acquisition.com's memos to embed into Tutu's playbook, translated for creative economy:

### The Constraint Identification Model
Develop a diagnostic process that maps creative founders to their primary constraint(s). This becomes the core of advisory work — just like Acquisition.com's L2 focuses on the "most significant constraint."

### The Memo Culture
Every major decision gets a written memo. Builds institutional knowledge, accountability, structural thinking. Tutu's Weekly Memos serve double duty: public thought leadership + internal discipline.

### The AI Intelligence Loop
Record/transcribe discovery calls, sales calls, client conversations. Use AI to identify patterns in language, constraints, buying triggers. Build messaging around the audience's exact words.

### The Iterative Org Design
Structure is a hypothesis, not a commitment. Test, measure, iterate. Split "running the business" from "building the business" even when both roles are filled by one person.

### The Book-as-Brand-Moment Strategy
Each future book encapsulates a key framework and serves as a brand moment. Not about book revenue — about positioning. Plan years in advance.
